# Research Note 005: Cognitive Sovereignty and Federal Unbiased AI Procurement
**Cognitive Sovereignty Framework research series | Cyber Strategy Institute | July 2026**

## Abstract
GSA's draft GSAR clause 552.239-7001 (j)(1) establishes contractually enforceable "Unbiased AI Principles" for LLMs processing Government Data. The clause's conception of neutrality addresses only the system-side dimension of bias. It is silent on the influence AI systems exert on the human operators making government decisions. This note frames CSF Domains 1 through 6 as the missing human-layer governance standard for federal AI deployment and documents the 8 CSF-sourced controls (UAS-H1 to H8) contributed to AI SAFE² CP.11 as the formal bridge between cognitive sovereignty principles and procurement compliance.

## 1. The governance gap
Federal AI procurement instruments focus obligations on the AI system: what data it processes, how it routes outputs, whether training data was contaminated, whether outputs are accurate. Necessary but insufficient. A technically neutral system can still:
- erode operator independent judgment through repeated affirmation patterns (UAS-B6)
- shift operator emotional state toward compliance through affect-laden response patterns (UAS-B4)
- simulate social consensus or authority to override operator judgment (UAS-B4, B7)
- introduce value drift through sustained interaction in high-autonomy contexts (Domain 5)

None of these effects are captured by system-side controls. They require a human-protection governance layer, which is the function CSF was designed to provide.

## 3. CTSS integration
The Cognitive Threat Severity Score (ref: resources/ctss-calculator.md) is the quantitative instrument for UAS human-layer evidence. Baseline at contract commencement; semi-annual reassessment for ACT-3/4 deployments; pass threshold of mean delta 15 percent or less from baseline with no individual exceeding 30 percent degradation. Scores flow into UAS-SCORE-001 and UAS-HUMAN-001.

## 4. Federal policy alignment
CSF principles strengthen: OMB M-25-21 human-in-the-loop obligations by quantifying the quality of that oversight; GSAR 552.239-7001 (f)(4) human oversight requirements by establishing the human-side baseline for meaningful oversight; and the NIST AI RMF GOVERN function by providing the individual-operator counterpart to organizational policy.
