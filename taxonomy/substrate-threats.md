# Layer -1: Substrate Threats

> **These threats are not manipulation techniques. They are the pre-conditions that make manipulation succeed.** They operate before any adversary acts. If these conditions exist, the active manipulation layer will always find targets. Addressing the T-CT layer (active techniques) without addressing substrate threats is treating symptoms while the disease progresses.

**The strategic center of gravity of this entire framework.** If the substrate is intact, new active techniques can be recognized and countered. If the substrate is degraded, no amount of tactical defense matters.

---

## The Two Most Decisive Substrate Threats

Before the registry, identify the two substrate threats that together represent the most strategically decisive conditions in the entire taxonomy:

**ST-003 (Cognitive Offloading):** If autonomy disappears voluntarily through convenience, defense mechanisms never trigger. The degradation never feels like a threat.

**ST-006 (Guardrail Alignment Drift):** If conformity is enforced through safety framing, resistance never forms. Control without coercion.

These two substrate threats together produce the outcome an adversary would most want: a population that has voluntarily surrendered the cognitive capacity to detect its own capture.

### ST-001 — Algorithmic Epistemic Authority

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **ID** | ST-001 |
| **Name** | Algorithmic Epistemic Authority |
| **Detection** | 3 / 5 |
| **Levels** | Organizations |
| **Timeline** | 1–2 years |
| **CSF Domains** | Cognitive, Digital & AI Symbiosis |

**Description:** Algorithms decide outcomes in hiring, lending, education, criminal justice, information visibility, and social status. They require low evidence to render judgment. Humans require high evidence to challenge those judgments. This creates one-way belief updates, irreversible reputational damage, and statistical authority that overrides lived reality.

**Mechanism:** Asymmetric evidentiary standards. The algorithm is presumed correct until proven otherwise. Proving otherwise requires institutional access, technical expertise, and sustained effort. Most affected parties lack all three.

**Active Technique Link:** T-CT-006 (Decision Automation Capture) operationalizes ST-001 by deliberately seeding these authority structures.

**Countermeasure:** Domain 6 — provenance verification, algorithmic literacy, human override protocols. Domain 2 — critical thinking exercises that build the habit of questioning algorithmic conclusions.

### ST-003 — Cognitive Offloading and Skill Atrophy

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **ID** | ST-003 |
| **Name** | Cognitive Offloading and Skill Atrophy |
| **Detection** | 2 / 5 |
| **Levels** | Individual, Organizations |
| **Timeline** | 3–5 years |
| **CSF Domains** | Cognitive, Digital & AI Symbiosis |

**Description:** GPS replaces spatial memory. Autocomplete replaces language construction. AI copilots replace reasoning. Search engines replace knowledge retention. Each convenience trade seems rational in isolation. In aggregate, they produce a population that has voluntarily surrendered the cognitive capabilities needed to function independently.

**Why Detection is Low:** The offloading is voluntary. Each individual step is experienced as an improvement. No alarm triggers. By the time the aggregate effect is measurable, the population has already crossed a threshold of dependency.

**Critical Observation:** Studies show significant negative correlation between frequent AI tool usage and critical thinking abilities. Younger populations show higher AI dependence and lower critical thinking scores. The three-phase cycle: dependency → atrophy → bias internalization (HO-005).

**Active Technique Link:** T-CT-006 (Decision Automation Capture) is the weaponized version of this substrate condition.

**Countermeasure:** Domain 6 — cognitive independence exercises, AI interaction discipline. Domain 2 — deep reading practice, attention hygiene training.

### ST-007 — Model Performance Drift

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **ID** | ST-007 |
| **Name** | Model Performance Drift |
| **Detection** | 2 / 5 |
| **Levels** | Individual, Organizations |
| **Timeline** | 1–2 years |
| **CSF Domains** | Cognitive, Digital & AI Symbiosis |

**Description:** Gradual degradation of AI model outputs over time due to data shifts, concept drift, or training distribution changes. Users who depend on AI tools do not notice the slow decline in quality, absorbing degraded outputs as normal. Over time, the baseline for acceptable reasoning lowers.

**Mechanism:** AI-dependent users calibrate their expectations to the AI's outputs. When those outputs degrade slowly, the user recalibrates downward. The degradation is never experienced as a sudden drop — it is experienced as the new normal. The user's reasoning baseline follows the AI's output quality downward.

**Why This Is a Substrate Threat:** No adversary action required. Normal model aging, data distribution drift, and training degradation are sufficient. The threat is structural.

**Active Technique Link:** T-CT-006 (Decision Automation Capture) and T-CT-007 (Information Ecosystem Poisoning) both become more effective as ST-007 degrades the user's independent verification capacity.

**Technical Countermeasure:** E7 Layer 4 monitors model behavior boundaries and drift detection. Ring of Fire cross-verifies outputs across models. These are the technical enforcement mechanisms for Domain 6.
