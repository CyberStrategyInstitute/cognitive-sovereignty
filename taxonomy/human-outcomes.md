# Layer +2: Human Outcome Effects

> **These are the measurement layer.** When threats succeed, they produce measurable changes in human cognition and behavior. These are not theoretical risks — they are observable patterns already present in the population. The delivery is technical. The impact is cognitive. The outcome manifests as physical change in behavior, society, and institutions.

---

## Registry

### HO-001 — Attention Ownership Loss

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Detection** | 4 / 5 |
| **Primary Techniques** | T-CT-001, T-CT-014 |
| **Primary Substrates** | ST-002 |

**Cognitive Impact:** The person no longer controls where their attention goes. Platforms, algorithms, and notification systems direct cognitive focus. Cognitive fragmentation reduces creative output quality by 30 to 50 percent. The shift from deep attention to hyperattention (multiple foci, rapid switching) is documented and accelerating.

**Physical/Societal Manifestation:** Reduced productivity, shortened task completion, exhaustion. Passive citizenry, low civic participation. A population that cannot sustain focused attention cannot engage in the deliberative processes democracy requires.

**Measurement:** Pre/post cognitive testing, task completion metrics, self-reported focus duration. Success threshold: 20–30% improvement in controlled studies using attention hygiene interventions.

### HO-002 — Belief Instability

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Detection** | 3 / 5 |
| **Primary Techniques** | T-CT-002, T-CT-004, T-CT-018 |
| **Primary Substrates** | ST-005 |

**Cognitive Impact:** The person can no longer hold stable beliefs under pressure. Rapid narrative reversals, contradictory claims, and synthetic media create an environment where truth feels unknowable. People shift from truth-seeking to tribe-seeking.

**Physical/Societal Manifestation:** Polarized communities, election disruptions, policy manipulation. False information spreads six times faster than truthful information on social platforms. Affective polarization has nearly doubled in the U.S. since the mid-1990s.

**Measurement:** Inoculation training effectiveness, pre/post exposure testing. Success threshold: ~40% reduction in false narrative adoption through inoculation training.

### HO-003 — Identity Fragmentation

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Detection** | 4 / 5 |
| **Primary Techniques** | T-CT-005, T-CT-016 |
| **Primary Substrates** | ST-002, ST-005 |

**Cognitive Impact:** The person's sense of self becomes platform-dependent. Recommendation systems co-author identity trajectories. Individuals high in identity coherence present authentic selves online. Those high in identity confusion present false or idealized selves, creating a feedback loop.

**Physical/Societal Manifestation:** Family dissolution, relationship instability, fertility decline. Heavy digital media users (5+ hours/day) are 48 to 171 percent more likely to exhibit low well-being or suicide risk factors. Incentive structures reward narcissistic traits over relational commitments.

**Civilizational Dimension:** Identity fragmentation is the individual-level expression of CD-006 (Civilizational Reproduction Failure). When enough individuals experience identity fragmentation, the civilization loses the capacity to transmit its organizing principles to the next generation.

### HO-004 — Moral Disengagement

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Detection** | 2 / 5 |
| **Primary Techniques** | T-CT-015, T-CT-006 |
| **Primary Substrates** | CD-001 |

**Cognitive Impact:** Moral reasoning capacity degrades or is redirected. Ethics get outsourced to automated systems. Responsibility diffuses. Convenience displaces virtue. The slow redefinition of right and wrong occurs through language shifts, policy framing, and cultural production.

**Physical/Societal Manifestation:** Ethical lapses in policy, biased automated decisions, norm erosion. Civilizations rarely fall by force; they reinterpret themselves into collapse.

**Detection Challenge (2/5):** Moral disengagement is experienced as moral progress. The redefinition of categories is experienced as enlightenment. The outsourcing of ethics to automated systems is experienced as efficiency.

### HO-005 — Agency Erosion

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Detection** | 3 / 5 |
| **Primary Techniques** | T-CT-006, T-CT-013 |
| **Primary Substrates** | ST-003, ST-006 |

**Cognitive Impact:** The person retains rights in theory but loses agency in practice. Freedom is traded for ease, safety, personalization, and automation. Studies show significant negative correlation between frequent AI tool usage and critical thinking abilities. Younger populations show higher AI dependence and lower critical thinking scores.

**Physical/Societal Manifestation:** Decision paralysis, workforce displacement, obedience without awareness. The three-phase cycle: dependency → atrophy → bias internalization.

**The Most Insidious Outcome:** Agency erosion is the outcome an adversary who controls cognition would most want to produce. A population that retains all nominal rights but lacks the cognitive capacity to exercise them independently is, in practice, a captured population.

**EFA Connection:** This is the human outcome that EFA (Ethical Functionality without Agency) is specifically designed to prevent at the system level. By refusing to attribute agency to AI systems, EFA prevents the gradual cognitive delegation that produces this outcome.

## Wargame Insight

Scenarios consistently showed that cognitive impacts produce physical outcomes:

- **Election disruption (HO-002)** results from belief instability, not from hacking voting machines
- **Workforce displacement (HO-005)** results from agency erosion, not from job automation alone
- **Family dissolution (HO-003)** results from identity fragmentation driven by incentive-captured platforms, not from cultural evolution alone

**The delivery is technical. The impact is cognitive. The outcome is physical change.**
