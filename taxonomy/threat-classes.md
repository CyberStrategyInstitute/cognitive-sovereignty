# Newly Identified Threat Classes (TC-01 through TC-10)

> **Why these were missing:** These threat classes operate through incentive structures, environmental conditioning, and voluntary adoption rather than through deliberate adversary action. Traditional threat models focus on what someone does to you. These classes address what the system does to itself. That is why they accounted for the unmodeled **20 to 30 percent** of modern cognitive influence.

---

## Overview

Ten additional threat classes were identified following the initial taxonomy development. They represent mechanisms that are distinct from existing CD, ST, and T-CT entries but were not fully captured because they:

1. Operate without an adversary (emergent from systemic incentives)
2. Are experienced as beneficial or neutral by the affected population
3. Produce cognitive effects through environmental shaping rather than targeted messages

---

## Registry

### TC-01 — Incentive Capture Systems

**Definition:** Structures that quietly reward specific beliefs or behaviors until they feel voluntary. No visible coercion. Career advancement, social approval, and funding access are tied to narrative conformity. Populations self-police.

**Mechanism:** If the reward gradient for conforming to a narrative is consistently higher than the reward gradient for independent judgment, the population will converge on the narrative without any explicit instruction. No adversary required.

**Detection:** Explains 20–30% of unattributed narrative convergence. Detect by mapping reward gradients across professional, social, and economic domains.

**Taxonomy Links:** CD-001, T-CT-010, T-CT-015

**Measurement:** Reward asymmetry for dissent versus conformity, monetization ratios of outrage versus nuance, career or social penalties tied to viewpoint expression.

---

### TC-02 — Reality Fluidity Mechanisms

**Definition:** Conditions where truth becomes unstable or unknowable. Rapid narrative reversals, conflicting expert claims, and synthetic media indistinguishability. Populations shift from truth-seeking to tribe-seeking.

**Mechanism:** When the cost of truth-seeking exceeds the cost of tribal affiliation, rational actors become tribal actors. This is not irrationality — it is a rational response to an environment where truth is not reliably accessible.

**Detection:** Track rate of epistemic foundation erosion — contradiction tolerance, narrative reversal speed, tribal identification ratios.

**Taxonomy Links:** CD-002, T-CT-014, HO-002

---

### TC-03 — Algorithmic Identity Shaping

**Definition:** Recommendation systems co-author identity trajectory. Not just opinions but lifestyle, values, relationships, political alignment, and self-worth. Identity becomes platform-dependent.

**Mechanism:** The recommendation algorithm knows more about the user's psychological profile than the user does. It uses this knowledge to serve content that reinforces and extends the identity it has already shaped. The process is circular and self-amplifying.

**Taxonomy Links:** ST-005, T-CT-005, HO-003

**Measurement:** Correlation analysis — identity markers versus platform recommendation patterns versus traditional formation pathways.

---

### TC-04 — Emotional Climate Engineering

**Definition:** Persistent shaping of baseline societal mood. Instead of persuading thoughts, systems tune anxiety, anger, hopelessness, moral panic, and urgency. Emotion determines what populations will tolerate.

**Key Insight:** A population in chronic anxiety accepts surveillance. In chronic anger, accepts conflict. In chronic hopelessness, accepts authoritarian promises. Emotional climate engineering pre-positions populations to accept specific solutions.

**Taxonomy Links:** T-CT-003, HO-002

**Measurement:** Population-level emotional baselines tracked longitudinally. Persistent elevation predicts susceptibility to extreme solutions.

---

### TC-05 — Cognitive Overload Saturation

**Definition:** Flooding attention until analysis collapses. Infinite news cycles, contradictory data streams, and performative crisis culture. People default to tribal heuristics, authority shortcuts, and apathy.

**Mechanism:** When the volume of incoming information exceeds analytical processing capacity, the mind defaults to shortcuts. The most available shortcuts are tribal affiliation and authority trust. Both produce predictable outputs.

**Taxonomy Links:** T-CT-014, HO-001

**Measurement:** Track information volume against decision quality. Rising volume paired with declining analytical engagement signals overload saturation.

---

### TC-06 — Synthetic Social Proof

**Definition:** Artificial consensus signals — bots, coordinated narratives, inflated metrics, AI-generated communities. Exploits the cognitive bias that equates widespread belief with truth.

**Mechanism:** Humans assess the validity of claims partly by how widely they appear to be held. If manufactured consensus signals can make a false claim appear widely held, the bias toward social proof will cause real humans to update toward the false claim.

**Taxonomy Links:** DS-002, T-CT-008

**Detection:** Bot-to-human interaction ratios, engagement topology analysis, narrative emergence without organic seed nodes (S1 — Synthetic Consensus Scanner).

---

### TC-07 — Moral Reframing Drift

**Definition:** Slow redefinition of right versus wrong, freedom versus safety, truth versus harm, debate versus danger. Occurs through language shifts, policy framing, education narratives, and cultural production.

**Mechanism:** Each individual reframing step appears small and reasonable. In aggregate, the moral landscape has shifted substantially. The population that accepted the final position would have rejected it if asked to accept it from the starting position.

**Taxonomy Links:** T-CT-015, HO-004

**Measurement:** Track velocity and direction of moral category redefinition in discourse, curricula, and legal frameworks. High velocity indicates instability.

---

### TC-08 — Institutional Legibility Collapse

**Definition:** Citizens cannot understand how decisions are made. Bureaucratic opacity, algorithmic governance, legal complexity, regulatory layering. Loss of trust, accountability, and civic participation.

**Reality Legibility Score:** Can citizens answer four questions about decisions affecting them?
1. Who made this decision?
2. By what authority?
3. Using what data?
4. With what appeal path?

Declining scores indicate drift toward authoritarian or technocratic governance — either by force or by complexity.

**Taxonomy Links:** CD-004, T-CT-009

---

### TC-09 — Autonomy Erosion by Convenience

**Definition:** Freedom traded for ease, safety, personalization, and automation. Gradual, voluntary, normalized. Rights retained in theory, agency lost in practice.

**Relationship to ST-003:** TC-09 is the macro-level phenomenon; ST-003 (Cognitive Offloading and Skill Atrophy) is the micro-level mechanism. TC-09 describes the overall pattern of voluntary autonomy surrender; ST-003 describes the specific cognitive capability loss.

**Taxonomy Links:** ST-003, HO-005

**Measurement:** Track percentage of cognitive tasks mediated by automation. Track skill atrophy rates. Track AI dependence versus independent decision-making.

---

### TC-10 — AI-Mediated Reality Layers

**Definition:** AI becomes the primary interpreter of the world. AI summaries replace reading, AI companions replace relationships, AI copilots replace judgment. Whoever shapes AI priors shapes civilization trajectory.

**Why This Is Distinct:** TC-10 is not about AI being used as a weapon (T-CT-006, T-CT-017). It is about AI becoming the default mediating layer between humans and reality. This can occur without any adversary intent — driven entirely by user convenience and platform economics.

**Taxonomy Links:** CD-003, ST-006, ST-007

**Measurement:** Human-versus-machine authority transfer rate. Trust in AI versus human sources. Percentage of knowledge acquisition through AI intermediaries.

**Critical Threshold:** When the majority of information consumed, social proof observed, and cultural trends engaged with are generated or mediated by AI systems, humans are making decisions inside a constructed reality. The decisions feel free. The reality they are based on is engineered.

---

## Coverage Impact

Adding TC-01 through TC-10 improved taxonomy coverage from approximately **80% to 92%** by capturing the emergent, systemic, and voluntary-adoption mechanisms that traditional adversary-focused threat models miss.

The remaining 8% gap falls into:
- Swarm adaptation speed exceeding detection capability
- Long-cycle civilizational effects (multi-generational)
- Genuinely novel mechanisms not yet observable
