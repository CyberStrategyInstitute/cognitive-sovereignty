# Domain 6 Self-Assessment Checklist
## Digital & AI Symbiosis — Individual and Unit Level

> **Purpose:** This checklist enables individual personnel and unit commanders to assess Domain 6 (Digital & AI Symbiosis) resilience. It is not a compliance exercise — it is a diagnostic tool. Honest answers are more valuable than good scores.

**How to use:** Complete independently. For unit-level assessment, aggregate individual results and identify the lowest-scoring capability areas. Those areas are your training priorities.

**Scoring:** 0 = Never / 1 = Rarely / 2 = Sometimes / 3 = Usually / 4 = Always

---

## Scoring Interpretation

| Total Score | Interpretation | Recommended Action |
|---|---|---|
| 80–100 | **Strong** — Domain 6 capabilities are well-developed | Maintain. Mentor others. |
| 60–79 | **Adequate** — Core capabilities present; specific gaps exist | Identify lowest-scoring sections. Target those for training. |
| 40–59 | **Developing** — Significant gaps requiring structured intervention | Complete Domain 6 training curriculum. Reassess in 60 days. |
| 20–39 | **At Risk** — Vulnerability across multiple Domain 6 capability areas | Priority training intervention. Limit unsupervised AI use for high-stakes decisions. |
| 0–19 | **Critical Gap** — Fundamental Domain 6 capabilities absent | Immediate training. Report to unit cognitive readiness officer. |

## Unit-Level Aggregation

For unit commanders, collect individual assessments and calculate:

1. **Unit average score** by section — identifies collective capability gaps
2. **Distribution of scores** — a bimodal distribution (high scorers and low scorers) indicates training is not reaching all personnel
3. **Lowest 20th percentile** — these personnel are the highest-risk cognitive security liability; prioritize for individual assessment
4. **Trend over time** — scores should improve with training; stable or declining scores after training indicate an ineffective training program
