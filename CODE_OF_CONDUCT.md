# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Cognitive security research requires rigorous, evidence-based discourse across ideological and institutional boundaries. We are committed to maintaining a community where contributors of all backgrounds can engage in honest, good-faith analysis without personal attack.

## Standards

Behavior that supports the mission:
- Evidence-based argumentation
- Honest identification of uncertainty and gaps
- Charitable interpretation of contributions before critique
- Willingness to update positions based on evidence
- Applying the same evidentiary standard to all claims regardless of political alignment

Behavior that undermines the mission:
- Personal attacks or harassment
- Ideological framing that encodes conclusions before examining evidence
- Coordinated campaigns to suppress specific viewpoints in the taxonomy
- Misrepresenting others' contributions or positions

## The Non-Ideological Standard

The framework's governing principle applies to contributors too: **we measure system properties, not belief content.** A contribution that applies different evidentiary standards based on the political valence of the conclusion will not be accepted, regardless of the contributor's intent.

## Enforcement

Violations may result in contribution rejection, temporary ban, or permanent ban depending on severity. Decisions are made by project maintainers.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns to the project maintainers through GitHub's reporting tools or by contacting Cyber Strategy Institute directly at cyberstrategyinstitute.com.
